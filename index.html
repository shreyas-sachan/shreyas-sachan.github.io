Shreyas Sachan

M.Sc. in Visual Computing

I am a Master of Science student in Visual Computing at Universität des Saarlandes, currently writing my master's thesis on "3D Mesh Reconstruction from a Single Event Camera." I have the privilege of conducting this research within the 4D and Quantum Computer Vision Group at the Visual Computing and Artificial Intelligence Department of Max Planck Institute for Informatics, under the expert guidance of Prof. Christian Theobalt and Dr. Vladislav Golyanik.

Before this, I was a Research Assistant at MPI-INF under Mohamed Elgharib, focusing on 3D face and hand capture, and a Project Scientist at IIT Delhi under Prof. Srikanta Bedathur and Prof. Maya Ramanath. Previously, I completed my Bachelor's thesis on neural network quantization at IIT Delhi under Prof. Kolin Paul, following my undergraduate studies at the Cluster Innovation Centre, University of Delhi.

Research Experience

Master's Thesis: 3D Mesh Reconstruction from a Single Event Camera

Max Planck Institute for Informatics

Developed EventNeuS, the first neural framework to extract high-quality 3D meshes solely from monocular event streams. This work introduced SDF-based representations for event data and utilized spherical harmonics to improve reconstruction quality, outperforming the previous state-of-the-art method with a 34% lower Chamfer distance.

Research Assistant

Max Planck Institute for Informatics (MPI-INF)

Designed and deployed a multi-view camera rig for high-precision 3D face and hand gesture capture. Implemented camera calibration pipelines and developed algorithms for synchronizing and processing multi-view visual data under the supervision of Mohamed Elgharib.
